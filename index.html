Understanding GST (Goods and Services Tax)

GST is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the manufacture, sale, and consumption of goods and services across India. It has replaced several indirect taxes like VAT, excise duty, and service tax.

Manufacturer
Wholesaler
Retailer
Consumer

Manufacturer: The manufacturer produces goods and pays GST on the raw materials they purchase. When they sell the product to the wholesaler, they charge GST.

Wholesaler: The wholesaler buys goods from the manufacturer and pays GST. When they sell the goods to the retailer, they again charge GST.

Retailer: The retailer purchases goods from the wholesaler and pays GST. When they sell the goods to the consumer, they charge GST, which the consumer pays.

Consumer: The final consumer pays GST on the final product. The tax paid by the consumer is the sum of the GST applied at each stage.
